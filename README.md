## 01. Technical Pillars of Development

The software and design logic of Nautilus are divided into functional areas:

### 1.1 Automation and Fabrication Module (Robotics/Assembly)
* **Autofabrication Algorithms:** Logic for the optimal assembly sequence of the spacecraft and subsequent replicators.

* **ISRU Engine Logic:** Code to orchestrate mining rovers and raw material extractors (e.g., silicate, metals) from asteroids or planets.

### 1.2 Long-Term Decision Module (Deep Time AI)
* **Mission Coherence Check:** AI algorithms that evaluate whether a decision (e.g., replicating *vs* building a communication antenna) compromises the original mission (I¬≤).

* **Neural Entropy:** Techniques to mitigate the degradation and behavioral drift of the AI ‚Äã‚Äãmodel over vast periods of time (analogous to Neural Annealing in quantum systems), crucial for extending the lifespan of intelligence.

### 1.3 Navigation and Extreme Physics Module (Astrodynamics)
* **Self-Correction Vectoring:** Navigation that compensates for mechanical failures and system entropy using redundancy.

* **Propulsion Logic:** Simulations of advanced propulsion (e.g., solar sails or fission propulsion) for interstellar travel (Space Migration).
